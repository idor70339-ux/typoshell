⌨️ TypoShell

Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026
TypoShell does not collect, store, or share any personal data. All text processing happens entirely within your browser, on your device.

What TypoShell does

TypoShell is a Chrome extension that converts mistyped text between English and Hebrew keyboard layouts. It detects wrong-language input and offers corrections as you type.

Data collection

TypoShell does not collect any user data. The table below summarises what is and is not accessed:

Data typeCollected?Notes
Text you type or select ❌ No Processed locally in memory only; never recorded or transmitted.
Browsing history or page content ❌ No Not accessed beyond the currently selected or typed text.
Personal information (name, email, etc.) ❌ No Never collected at any point.
Individual words (dictionary check) ⚠️ Limited A single word at a time is sent to dictionaryapi.dev to verify whether it is valid English. No surrounding text, no page URL, and no identifying information is included.
User preferences (feature toggles) ✅ Locally only Your on/off settings are stored in chrome.storage.sync on your device. This data never leaves Google's infrastructure and is not accessible to TypoShell's developer.

Data handling

All text analysis — language detection, keyboard-layout conversion, and auto-correct suggestions — is performed entirely in your browser using JavaScript. No text is sent to any server operated by TypoShell or its developer.

Data storage

TypoShell stores only your feature preferences (on/off toggles) using the browser's built-in chrome.storage.sync API. This data:

  • Contains no personal information — only boolean on/off flags.
  • Is stored by Chrome on your device and optionally synced via your Google account, subject to Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Can be cleared at any time by removing the extension.

No other data is written to disk, localStorage, cookies, or any external database.

Data sharing

TypoShell does not share any user data with third parties, with one narrow exception:

  • dictionaryapi.dev — When auto-correct is active, a single word is sent to the free dictionaryapi.dev API to check whether it is valid English. Only the isolated word is transmitted — no surrounding text, no page content, no cookies, and no identifying information.

No data is sold, rented, or shared with advertisers or analytics platforms.

Permissions explained

  • activeTab — Required to read selected or typed text on the current page in order to offer corrections.
  • scripting — Required to inject the correction toolbar and suggestion UI into web pages.
  • storage — Used to save your feature-toggle preferences.
  • clipboardWrite — Used only to copy converted text when the target element is not directly editable.
  • host_permissions (<all_urls>) — Required so the extension can operate on any website, not just a predefined list.

Children's privacy

TypoShell does not knowingly collect any information from children under the age of 13, nor is it directed at children.
